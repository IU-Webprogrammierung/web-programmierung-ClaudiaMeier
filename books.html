I am currently reading

Bodo Schaefer - The Laws of the Winners

The Laws of the Winners by Bodo Schafer has helped innumerable people, and you can be one of them!What is it that you want most out of life? Is it wealth, power, or even happiness perhaps? One's future is up in the air for many people, and they just let the current of life sweep them any which way. Wouldn't it be great if there were a set of rules to follow, laws per se, that could help you join the ranks of the productive and successful? As it turns out, there are. Bodo Schafer is a successful time-management trainer. Through his time spent as a financial guru, Schafer has come up with a set of thirty laws that, when followed, can drastically improve the quality of your life. These laws give you the tools you need to gain control of your life and attain the confidence you need to move forward with all the purpose and gusto of a true winner.In this book, you will learn how to be happy, smart, and successful; the tools needed to achieve your dreams; the secret to having immeasurable confidence; and so much more!There's no easy fix when it comes to achieving success. There is, however, a series of steps you can follow to ensure that your life improves in a dramatic and measurable way. These laws have assisted many over a lengthy period of time, and this infallible method can help you too!

My last book

Michelle Adams - Hidden Treasures

Once upon a time, in a small village in southern France, a pretty, willful English girl is falling in love. Frances Langley has fallen under Benoit's romantic spell, so sure is she that he is everything she's ever wanted—a self-assured, sexy man, experienced and just a little bit mysterious. But Frances is hiding a secret—one that would surely separate them if he ever knew the truth. And to hold on to his love, she is willing to do anything for him, even put herself at risk by hiding a precious object, stolen by the Nazis decades before. Years later, Frances's son, Harry, opens the door of his late mother's home, never expecting to see Tabitha—the lost love of his life—on the other side. Their angry parting had broken his heart, but now she holds a letter, sent by his mother just before her death, begging the pair to search—together—for a priceless jewelry box, hidden somewhere in her little Cotswold cottage. Harry quickly dismisses the search, but as an art historian, Tabitha cannot risk the chance to recover something so valuable that was long thought to be lost. And so they embark on a journey of discovery, but soon find themselves searching for much more than a missing piece of art. Together they learn that the true riches are not those buried in the clutter of Francis' cottage, but are instead the treasures they each hold, buried deep inside their hearts.

My Recommendations

Paulo Coelho - The Alchemist

Combining magic, mysticism, wisdom, and wonder into an inspiring tale of self-discovery, The Alchemist has become a modern classic, selling millions of copies worldwide and transforming the lives of countless readers. Paulo Coelho's masterpiece tells the mystical story of Santiago, an Andalusian shepherd boy who longs to travel in search of a worldly treasure. His quest leads him to riches far different—and far more fulfilling — than he ever imagined. Santiago's journey teaches us the wisdom of listening to our hearts, recognizing opportunity, and learning to read the omens along life's path. Most importantly, it shows us the value of following our dreams.

Brianna Wiest - The Mountain Is You

This book explores the concept of self-sabotage—why we do it, when we do it, and how to break free from it for good. Conflicting needs create self-sabotaging behaviors, often making change seem futile. But by gaining insight into our damaging habits, understanding our brains and bodies, releasing past traumas, and learning to act as our highest selves, we can move beyond our own obstacles. For centuries, the mountain has symbolized the big challenges we face, especially those that seem insurmountable. To climb these mountains, we must do the deep internal work of healing, building resilience, and adjusting how we approach life. In the end, we do not master the mountain, but ourselves.

Brianna Wiest - 101 Essays that will change the way you think

101 Essays That Will Change The Way You Think, the global bestseller and social media sensation, is a carefully curated collection of Brianna Wiest's most popular and influential writings. Her essays offer profound reflections on why you should prioritize purpose over passion, embrace negative thinking, see the value in daily routines, and become aware of the cognitive biases that shape your perception of the world. Some of these essays have never been published before, while others have already been read by millions across the globe. No matter the piece, each will leave you reflecting deeply: this idea changed my life.
